# Seam Discipline — Seam Comparison Records

**Authority:** James Aull / MagicianzCardstock LLC
**Status:** Active — accepting authorized seam comparison records

---

## Purpose

Seam Comparison Records document the result of applying the Seam Discipline Declaration Intake Profile to two independently governed layers that operate at the same or adjacent consequential boundary.

Each record shows how two layers can be compared without one absorbing the other — enforcement participant and independent witness, substrate and witness layer, control and attestation — while preserving the non-overlapping authority surfaces and explicit non-claims of both sides.

**The Seam Discipline is credited as the reviewer-side comparison method that makes each record possible.**

---

## What a Seam Comparison Record Is

A Seam Comparison Record is:

- **Mutual** — both layer owners authorize the exact language before publication
- **Bilateral** — each side declares its own boundary in its own terms
- **Bounded** — constrained to the seam between the two layers, with no integration instructions, endorsement, or authority transfer
- **Reviewer-side** — produced for external reviewers, not for runtime use

A Seam Comparison Record is not:

- An endorsement of either layer by the other
- A partnership, integration agreement, or shared product
- A claim that the two layers together form a complete or certified governance stack
- A representation that either layer adopts the Seam Discipline framework

---

## Authorization Requirement

No Seam Comparison Record is published in this folder without explicit authorization from both layer owners for the exact language appearing in the record.

Draft records in progress are not published here until both owners confirm final language.

## How to Request a Seam Comparison Record

If you are a layer owner who has completed a Companion Layer Profile and wants to document a clean seam between your layer and ASRO or another listed layer, contact james@aisystemsreliability.org with "Seam Comparison Record" in the subject line.

The process:
1. Both layer owners exchange non-confidential boundary declarations
2. A draft record is produced using the Declaration Intake Profile field structure
3. Both owners review and authorize exact language
4. The record is published only after both owners confirm final language
